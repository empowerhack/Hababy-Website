The App

Based on trimesters

General advice is given depending on the current month/trimester of pregnancy.

Symptoms

Symptoms or distress can be found within the app, and actions to take (ideally represented as pictures) and what to tell your doctor is suggested.

Countries

Country-dependent information can be given including the eligibility to free support and generic medicine names.

Cooperations

Additional information can be given in a crowd-funded way.

How it Works

HaBaby, named from combining the Arabic word “habibi,” meaning sweetheart or baby, with “baby,” strives to support pregnant refugees in transit.

HaBaby focuses on the ability to capture key symptoms and a lightweight patient history. Features include: a limited decision tree to triage the top 5 most life-threatening symptoms, patient history, offline usage, all with a focus on visual communication. It is accessible in multiple languages (English and Arabic to start). We try to provide better patient-doctor communication in essential triage situations, increase the number and efficiency of people supported by constrained medical personnel (decreasing situations where common ailments are incorrectly escalated), clarify information, use design to build trust in refugees and promote communication with their medical personnel, and lower mortality and morbidity rates for women and children.

Background

HaBaby is an initiatve supported by EmpowerHack. EmpowerHack is a collective that creates sustainable design for women and girls in humanitarian contexts. Bridging the gap between technology, design, and NGOs to address key challenges, EmpowerHack galvanises volunteers to create code that crosses borders. Despite up to 80% of world’s refugees being women and children, the problems faced by them are never at the forefront. EmpowerHack was born to change just that by taking collaborative design to scale. Female refugees are among the most vulnerable, facing sexual violence, coercion, forced and early marriage, social isolation, and a lack of access to gender-specific health care and education.
